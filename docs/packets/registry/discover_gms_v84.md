The gms_v84 registry was seeded as a **copy of the v83 CSV column** (there is no
v84 CSV column; task-083 found the v84 *login/version handshake* byte-identical
to v83). This discovery pass tested that copy against the v84 IDB and found the

**inserts new clientbound opcodes** at several points, producing a *cumulative
additive shift* that grows with the opcode value.

This is the "LARGE delta = investigate, do not apply blindly" case. A blind
`--apply` is impossible anyway (the shift manifests as a flood of opcode
collisions, which the tool refuses to auto-apply), and even a hand reshift is
out of scope for a discovery pass because most v84 IDB handlers are unnamed
`sub_XXXX` (no fname to key a re-derivation on).
